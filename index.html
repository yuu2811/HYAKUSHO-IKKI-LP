While visiting growers and wholesale markets nationwide with Co-founder Kubo during my time at User Like, I was deeply impressed by the passion and craftsmanship of the seniors who have supported Japan's primary industries. At the same time, I realized the industry was piled with issues. We established the Flower Digital Marketing Consorthium and conducted research on cost improvement and structural issues with support from MAFF.

In an industry with remarkably few young producers, I decided to become a next-generation producer myself. I started by cultivating 50 tsubo of abandoned farmland. Continuing the restoration, we expanded to 500 tsubo. Securing an additional ~2,400 tsubo in Numazu City, Shizuoka, I decided to found HYAKUSHO-IKKI. In Japan, an advanced aging society, we will create a system where youth and seniors unite.
